Bulimia Nervosa

Bulimia is a illness that is serious and can be life threatening to one’s life or someone else. Self vomiting is caused by the feeling of disgust by binge eating.

Bulimia Anorexia

A serious behavioral issue of lack of appetite of food

Symptoms

  • Episodes of secretly binge eating
  • Self-induced vomiting
  • Body Image disturbance
  • Feelings of guilt or shame about eating
  • Depression and irritability
  • Skips meals or eats less than normal
  • Exercises more than normal to make up for the food that was ate

STATISTICS

  • Bulimia statistics tell us that the lifetime prevalence of bulimia nervosa in the United States is 1.5% in women and 0.5% in men meaning approximate 4.7 million females and 1.5 million males have Bulimia.
  • 1 in 10 patients that suffer from bulimia also have a substance disorder including alcohol use etc.
  • 0.9% of American women suffer from anorexia in their lifetime.
  • More than half of bulimia patients have comorbid anxiety disorders.
