Suicide/Depression

You Matter.

About:

The reason I chose this topic because I want to make people feel more secure and want them to feel that their privacy is not being invaded in this website. I care about this topic because I know the feeling loss interest in activities and knowing how I would direct violence at myself. Looking at other website that “helps” us. It’s telling us we need help and some helps does “work” for others, but what happens to the rest of the people that are going through same situation and the “help” there looking for never seem to work for them. I want this website to help the people that are going through this situation. I want everyone to be free and share what's on their mind and be comfortable to open up. Nobody is perfect. This website would be different because I want you guys the audience to feel that the community is safe and you’re not alone.





Public Service Announcement:

You Existing is a good thing and you have family and friends that love you. Don’t forget that you matter and that you are loved. You are good enough. Never think of the past that has hurt you and always think that it does get better. Like the Incredibles said “I never look back, darling. It distracts me from the now”.





Help Someone:

Want to help, but not sure what to do?

When people are facing depression and having suicidal thoughts, it’s never easy to talk to someone. It’s hard for people to open up and understand that it’s not easy. You want to make people feel safe and not feel like they’re being interrogated. For example, when approaching a scared animal you do wanna wait for it to come to you. But you don’t want to push it and make them runway. People with depression and suicidal thoughts, don’t want to talk about it. They want to make sure that they’re in the right place to be able to open up.Here’s what you could do:

  • Make them feel comfortable and not scared
  • Be able to not automatically mention the elephant in the room
  • Listen more than talk. Really listen closely to understand how they’re feeling
  • Think before you say something that could offend someone
  • Your feeling aren’t important and don’t make this about you
  • Make sure that the person is okay at the end of the conversation
